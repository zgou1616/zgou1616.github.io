Artificial intelligence for complex systems

Building reliable learning systems across AI and engineering.

I develop data-driven methods for complex physical and operational systems. My work spans trustworthy AI agents, stochastic modeling, scientific machine learning, and uncertainty quantification for mechanics and combustion.

I am currently an Applied Scientist at Amazon, where I work on reliable, scalable AI systems. I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Southern California in 2025, advised by Professor Roger Ghanem. I also hold master's degrees in Computer Science and Statistics from USC.

Continuing research in probabilistic learning, physical discovery, and trustworthy AI.

Research interests

Methods that remain useful when systems are uncertain, multiscale, or high-stakes.

01

Trustworthy AI systems

Reliable agentic workflows, knowledge-grounded generation, validation, evaluation, and scalable deployment.

02

Scientific machine learning

Data-efficient surrogate models, reduced-order modeling, localized physical discovery, and interpretable learning.

03

Uncertainty quantification

Switching diffusions, latent stochastic dynamics, multiscale uncertainty propagation, and rare-event prediction.

04

AI for engineering

Learning and inference for mechanics, materials, turbulence, combustion chemistry, and detonation dynamics.
