About

I am an applied scientist and researcher working at the intersection of artificial intelligence, computational science, and engineering. My research develops reliable learning methods for complex systems, with an emphasis on scientific machine learning, uncertainty quantification, and stochastic modeling.

I received my PhD in Engineering from the University of Southern California, where I worked with Professor Roger Ghanem on computational science for physical systems. I currently build reliable, scalable AI systems at Amazon while continuing research in probabilistic learning and physical discovery.

Research interests

  • Scientific machine learning and reduced-order modeling
  • Uncertainty quantification and stochastic dynamics
  • Trustworthy AI systems and agentic workflows
  • Computational methods for mechanics, materials, and combustion
