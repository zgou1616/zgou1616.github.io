Hi, there! I’m Zheming. I am an Applied Scientist working on machine learning and agentic AI systems. My work focuses on developing reliable, scalable AI solutions for business automation, fraud and integrity, and scientific applications.

I received my Ph.D. from the University of Southern California. My doctoral research studied uncertainty quantification, stochastic multiscale systems, surrogate modeling, switching diffusion processes, generative models, and high-performance computation.

I aim to connect state-of-the-art artificial intelligence with engineering and scientific fields. I am particularly interested in building AI systems that are reliable, interpretable, scalable, and grounded in physical or operational constraints.

▶ Agentic AI: reliable LLM workflows, validation, tool use, and scalable deployment.

▶ Scientific ML: surrogate modeling, stochastic dynamics, and uncertainty quantification.

▶ Generative Models: diffusion models for physical fields and scientific data.

▶ AI for Engineering: applying modern AI methods to mechanics, combustion, and simulation.

✉ I am open to research collaboration in AI agents, diffusion models, and scientific machine learning.

Research Interests

  • Agentic AI Systems: generation, validation, reasoning, orchestration, and evaluation.
  • Scientific Machine Learning: data-driven modeling for stochastic and multiscale systems.
  • Diffusion Models: generative modeling for flow fields and physical systems.
  • Uncertainty Quantification: switching diffusion, surrogate modeling, and reduced-order methods.
  • Computational Engineering: combustion, detonation, and simulation-driven discovery.
